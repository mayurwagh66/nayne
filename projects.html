Education for All

Project Shiksha: Igniting Young Minds

Education is the most potent weapon to change the world. However, thousands of children in low-income urban clusters and rural suburbs miss out on school due to unaffordable stationery, uniforms, or study resources.

Project Shiksha focuses on building interactive educational camps directly in slums and rural areas. We bridge learning deficits and help children integrate into primary school systems.

  • Distributed 8,000+ notebooks, school bags, and pencil boxes.
  • Setup 12+ weekend learning camps run by student mentors.
  • Sponsored formal school admission fees for 250+ kids.
  • Provided free evening tuition for grade 1 to 8 students.

Zero Hunger

Project Annapurna: Fighting Malnutrition

No child should have to study on an empty stomach. Hunger and malnutrition severely impact cognitive development in growing children. During the lockdowns, feeding families became an emergency necessity, which we have now institutionalized as a regular project.

Our team cooks and distributes freshly made, hot, and hygienic meals directly to construction worker camps, brick-kiln clusters, and remote villages.

  • Served over 50,000+ hot nutritional meals since inception.
  • Distributes dry ration boxes (rice, flour, dal, oil) to needy single-mother households.
  • Conducts special sweet and fruit distribution drives during major Indian festivals.
  • Runs stray animal feeding drives in town areas.
Sponsor Slum Family Meals (₹2,500)
Health & Dignity

Project Nari: Menstrual Hygiene & Awareness

In rural India, silence and taboos around menstruation prevent girls from attending school and lead to severe infections. Project Nari tackles the stigma head-on through health education workshops and regular pad distribution.

We work in collaboration with female health educators to teach girls about menstrual biology, hygiene care, and sanitary disposal methods.

  • Conducted 45+ menstrual health and reproductive awareness camps.
  • Distributed 15,000+ packs of high-quality biodegradable sanitary napkins.
  • Supported rural schools by helping install sanitary pad vending machines.
  • Provided vocational stitching classes to women to support livelihood.
Sponsor a Hygiene Kit for 5 Girls (₹500)
